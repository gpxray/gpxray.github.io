Downhill Load ⓘ Descent Load (DDL/km)
Measures downhill stress (steepness + time + surface).

We use cumulative DDL to estimate downhill pace loss.

Fatigue ratio = cumulative DDL ÷ DFT

Pace loss on descents:
• <0.8: none
• 0.8-1.0: +5-15 sec/km
• 1.0-1.2: +15-30 sec/km
• >1.2: +30-60 sec/km - How it works

Elevation Gain -

⬇️ Dynamic Descent Load → Pace Loss

DDL builds up as you descend. When cumulative DDL approaches your DFT, quads fatigue → downhill pace drops.

Ratio <0.8 Fresh legs → No pace loss
Ratio >1.0 Late race fatigue → +15-30 sec/km

💡 DFT = your downhill fatigue threshold (depends on runner level). Change level above to personalize.

Show formula

paceLoss = basePace × (1 + max(0, ratio–0.8)²) − basePace

This is a heuristic model, not medical advice.
